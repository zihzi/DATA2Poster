Sleep Health and Lifestyle Analysis

This analysis explores the relationships between sleep patterns, lifestyle factors, and health outcomes based on data from 374 individuals with various occupations and demographics.

Key Insights

Sleep Disorders

Percentage of participants with diagnosed sleep disorders, with Sleep Apnea (17.4%) being more common than Insomnia (13.3%).

Physical Activity

59.3

Average physical activity level (out of 100), with a strong positive correlation (0.68) with sleep quality and negative correlation (-0.56) with stress levels.

Detailed Findings

Our analysis reveals significant correlations between lifestyle factors and sleep health:

  • Occupation and Sleep: High-stress occupations like Sales Representatives and Software Engineers show lower sleep duration and quality compared to Doctors and Engineers.
  • BMI and Sleep Disorders: Individuals classified as Obese have a 4.5x higher prevalence of sleep apnea compared to those with Normal BMI.
  • Physical Activity Impact: Participants with high physical activity levels (>70) reported 32% better sleep quality scores and 41% lower stress levels.
  • Gender Differences: Males show higher prevalence of sleep apnea (19.6% vs 15.3% in females), while females report higher rates of insomnia (16.9% vs 10.1% in males).
  • Age Correlation: Sleep disorder prevalence increases with age, with a notable jump after age 50, particularly for sleep apnea.

Conclusion

This analysis highlights the critical relationship between lifestyle factors and sleep health. Physical activity emerges as a powerful protective factor against sleep disorders, while stress level appears to be a key risk factor. Occupation-specific interventions may be valuable, particularly for high-stress jobs that show poorer sleep outcomes. The data suggests that targeting weight management and stress reduction could significantly improve sleep quality across all demographics.
